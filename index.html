Texas State Law — Who Needs an Emissions Test?

Under Texas law, only gasoline-powered vehicles aged 2 to 24 years old registered in a designated emissions county (like Bexar County) are required to pass an annual emissions test. Brand new vehicles are exempt for their first 2 years. Vehicles 25 years old or older are completely exempt from emissions testing. If your vehicle falls in the 2–24 year range and is registered here, you must pass every year — no exceptions.

🌎 Out-of-State Insurance Accepted

If you're transferring your title to Texas or getting TX plates, your out-of-state insurance is perfectly fine to use — as long as it meets Texas minimum liability requirements:

  • $30,000 bodily injury per person
  • $60,000 bodily injury per accident
  • $25,000 property damage per accident

Most standard U.S. policies meet or exceed these limits — check your declarations page or call your insurer if unsure.

Tex. Transp. Code § 601.072 · Tex. Ins. Code § 1952.001

What If I FAILED?

Most vehicles pass on the first try. If yours doesn't, we tell you exactly why — and exactly what it takes to get it fixed. One fee covers it: your reinspection is free within 15 days.

🔧

Diagnostic Code (DTC)

Your vehicle's computer flagged an emissions-related fault. This needs to be diagnosed and repaired by a mechanic before you can pass. Once fixed, come back for your free reinspection.

⏳

Monitors Not Ready

Your vehicle's onboard systems haven't completed their self-checks — usually happens after a battery disconnect or recent repair. Drive the vehicle for a few days through normal stop-and-go cycles, then return.

⛽

Gas Cap Test Failed

A loose or faulty gas cap causes evaporative emissions to leak. Pick up a new gas cap at any auto parts store and come back — it's one of the quickest fixes.

📋

You pay once. Reinspection is free.

You're charged for the inspection when it's performed. If your vehicle fails, you have 15 days to make the necessary repairs and return for a free reinspection — no additional charge.

Tex. Transp. Code § 548.407 · § 548.509
