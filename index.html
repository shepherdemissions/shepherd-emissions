If Your Vehicle Doesn't Pass

What Happens If You FAIL

Most vehicles pass on the first try. But if yours doesn't, here's exactly what it means — and what to do next.

🔧

Diagnostic Code (DTC)

Your vehicle's computer flagged an emissions-related fault. This needs to be diagnosed and repaired by a mechanic before you can pass. Once fixed, come back for your free reinspection.

⏳

Monitors Not Ready

Your vehicle's onboard systems haven't completed their self-checks — usually happens after a battery disconnect or recent repair. Drive the vehicle for a few days through normal stop-and-go cycles, then return.

⛽

Gas Cap Test Failed

A loose or faulty gas cap causes evaporative emissions to leak. Pick up a new gas cap at any auto parts store and come back — it's one of the quickest fixes.

📋

You pay once. Reinspection is free.

You're charged for the inspection when it's performed. If your vehicle fails, you have 15 days to make the necessary repairs and return for a free reinspection — no additional charge.
